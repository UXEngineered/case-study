Fast, erodes trust

Right Balance

The Spine: a layout that encodes a mental model
4 min
Three columns. Read left to right to understand. Right to left to audit. The layout is a directional argument.
The Source Panel
Typography hierarchy signals the type of input: interview, research, technical analysis. Semantic status pills show confidence and recency. Density enables scanning hundreds of sources without overwhelm.
The Lineage Panel
Upstream traces sources. Downstream traces artifacts. Placed on the right because that's where your eye goes when you're questioning something — you start from the output and trace backward. The layout makes that instinct correct.

Design Judgment
Three decisions worth discussing.
Decision 01
Why can't agents promote to canonical?
Trust is earned incrementally, not granted by default. More automation feels like more value — but there's a category of decision where the cost of getting it wrong is high enough that it has to be a human call. The agent ceiling isn't a limitation. It's the design.
Decision 02
Why does reverberation flag instead of auto-update?
Automated propagation sounds efficient. It's wrong. When an upstream assumption changes, the right downstream response depends on context the system doesn't have. The flag model is slower — but it preserves judgment at exactly the moment judgment matters.
Decision 03
Why are movement events structural, not a feature?
I built the audit trail before most of the UI. If you treat auditability as a feature you add at the end, you get incomplete coverage. For agentic systems, humans need to see everything the agent did. Not a summary. Everything.
